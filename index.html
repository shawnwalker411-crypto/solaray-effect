Home Mining Profitability Calculator — Built for Residential Setups

Most Bitcoin mining calculators are designed for warehouse-scale operations running on industrial power contracts. This calculator is different — it's built specifically for home miners running ASIC hardware on residential electricity in North America. It accounts for real-world factors that generic calculators ignore: dual electricity rates with separate peak and off-peak pricing, solar power generation hours, free overnight electricity from utility buyback programs, and battery storage windows.

What Makes This Calculator Different

Dual electricity rates let you model setups where power costs change by time of day — common with solar buyback programs where you get free or credited electricity during solar production hours and pay grid rates at night. Solar hours, free night/weekend hours, and battery hours are separate inputs so you can precisely model your actual power situation rather than using a single blended rate. Loki Kit support calculates profitability for running 240V industrial miners like the Antminer S19 Pro or S21 on standard 120V household outlets at reduced power using aftermarket firmware.

Supported Hardware

The calculator includes over 60 ASIC miners across six algorithms: SHA-256 for Bitcoin (Antminer S19, S19 Pro, S19J Pro, S21, Whatsminer M50, M60 series), Scrypt for Litecoin and Dogecoin (L3++, L7, Mini-DOGE), KHeavyHash for Kaspa (KS0, KS1, KS2, KS3, KS5, KA Box), Etchash for Ethereum Classic, Equihash for Zcash, and X11 for Dash. Browse the full list in our Miner Catalog. Each miner's stock specifications are pre-loaded — select your hardware and adjust power settings to match your actual configuration. Live cryptocurrency prices update automatically via CoinGecko API. Track network stats in the Data Vault.

Solar Buyback vs. Mining: What's Your Excess Energy Worth?

If you have rooftop solar, your utility probably offers a buyback rate for excess energy you export to the grid. Many solar buyback plans pay between $0.02 and $0.06 per kWh. That means a system producing 25 kWh of excess during solar hours earns you roughly $0.50 to $1.50 per day selling back to the grid — and that rate is locked. It never grows. Now consider this: that same 25 kWh powering an efficient ASIC miner earns Bitcoin that changes in value every day. At today's prices, that's roughly $1.50 to $1.70 per day — but Bitcoin is a store of value. The BTC you mine today could be worth significantly more in a year. A $0.03 buyback credit will always be worth $0.03.

This is the real advantage of home mining with solar: your electricity cost during production hours is effectively $0.00/kWh. Combine that with a free nights or free weekends plan, and you can run miners 16—24 hours a day at zero or near-zero power cost. The calculator above lets you model this exact scenario — enter your solar hours, free night hours, and paid grid rate separately to see how much Bitcoin you'd stack compared to selling energy back at buyback rates. Miners on free nights and solar buyback utility plans are already doing this. Use the miner catalog to compare which hardware fits your solar production window, and check the Data Vault for live Bitcoin difficulty and pricing to see if the math works for your setup today.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Bitcoin mining still profitable at home in 2026? It depends entirely on your electricity cost. Home miners with solar power, free overnight electricity, or rates below $0.08/kWh can still mine profitably with efficient hardware like the S19K Pro or S21. Use this calculator with your actual rates to find out.

Can I run an Antminer S19 on a regular outlet? Not at full power — S19 models require 240V. But with a Loki Kit and aftermarket firmware (LuxOS, VNish, or Braiins OS+), you can run one hash board on a standard 120V outlet at approximately 40% hashrate and 1,200W power draw. See our Electrical Setup Guide for wiring details.

What is a solar buyback program? Some utility companies credit your account for excess solar energy exported to the grid, effectively giving you free electricity during daytime hours. This calculator lets you input those free solar hours separately from your paid grid rate to see exactly how solar offsets your mining costs.
